I. Historical Background:

Barangay Malpalon was formerly called "Meypalon" by its Batangan (Tao-Buid) inhabitants. In the Tao-Buid dialect, Meypalon refers to a spring surrounded by thick, tall, and sturdy trees. The barangay is located in the northwest part of the Calintaan Municipality, bordered by Barangay Poypoy to the east, Barangay Concepcion to the west, Barangay Iriron to the south, and Barangay Burgos in Sablayan to the north.

The barangay was originally inhabited by the Tao-Buid Mangyan Sub-Tribe, who were later joined by lowland settlers attracted to its potential as pasture land. In 1968, Meypalon was officially renamed Malpalon and was designated as a barangay. A group of settlers, led by the late Efraim Tejada Sr., developed the area into agricultural land, and Efraim Tejada Sr. became the first Barangay Captain.

The barangay proper received its first jeepney in the 1980s, and to this day, it remains the only vehicle with access to the barangay proper. Sitio Balangabong, located 3 kilometers from the barangay proper, is home to 56 families, while Sitio Ulango houses 32 families, and Sitio Mamot is home to 12 families‚Äîall members of the Tao-Buid Mangyan Sub-Tribe.

With a land area of 24,448 hectares, Malpalon is the second-largest barangay. As of 2019, the estimated population was 2,865 according to BNS and BHW records. It is located about 13 kilometers from Calintaan's town proper, a journey that takes roughly half an hour by jeep or truck. The barangay has been electrified, and approximately 200 families benefit from this service.
